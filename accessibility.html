Accessibility Guide

Understanding accessibility features on London's transport network

Understanding Accessibility Symbols

Our journey planner uses the following symbols to indicate the level of accessibility at each station. Understanding these symbols will help you plan your journey more effectively.

♿ Full Step-Free Access

These stations have complete step-free access from street to platform. This means:

  • Level access from street to ticket hall
  • Lifts or ramps to all platforms
  • Suitable for wheelchair users
  • No steps or gaps to navigate

↔️ Interchange Access

These stations have step-free access between platforms, but may have steps from street level:

  • Easy transfers between lines
  • Level access between platforms
  • May require staff assistance
  • May have steps at station entrance

⚡ Partial Step-Free Access

These stations have some step-free access, but not to all platforms or areas:

  • Some platforms are step-free
  • Some routes may require steps
  • Check TfL website for specific platform information
  • May need to plan route carefully

⚠️ No Step-Free Access

These stations do not have step-free access and may present challenges:

  • Steps between street and platforms
  • No lift access available
  • Not suitable for wheelchair users
  • Staff assistance may be limited

Additional Information

When planning your journey, keep these important accessibility factors in mind:

  • Real-time updates: Always check TfL's live updates for lift availability
  • Staff assistance: Staff assistance is available at all stations
  • Advance notice: Some stations may require advance notice for ramp assistance
  • Platform gaps: Platform gap fillers are available at many stations
  • Accessible facilities: Many stations have accessible toilets and other amenities
